3D‑Printed Rotatable Amp Stand

Material: PLA • Mass target: ~280 g • Load: ~5.5 kg (12 lb)

Designed to raise and tilt a compact guitar amplifier, this 3D‑printed stand uses a low‑profile wedge and captive hardware to redirect sound toward the musician. The rotating hub incorporates an internal stop and nyloc‑secured pivot so the amp stays put at any angle. Ribbing and tuned infill concentrate strength only where it’s needed, keeping print time and mass to a minimum.

Gallery

Print & Assembly Notes

  • Nozzle 0.4 mm, layer height 0.2–0.28 mm; 4–5 walls; 10–20% grid/gyroid infill
  • Orient wedge on its side to maximize layer strength through the hinge area
  • Use TPU feet or adhesive pads to prevent creep on smooth floors
  • M5 countersunk bolt with nyloc nut provides a smooth, secure pivot

Design Highlights

  • Low profile pivot with captured hardware
  • Ribbed internal structure on compression side
  • Optional lock positions for fixed angles
  • Weight-efficient topology prints in roughly 280 g of PLA

Future Improvements

  • Detent mechanism for repeatable angle settings
  • Rubberized feet for added vibration isolation
  • Parametric scaling for different amplifier footprints
