Landing Gear Mechanism Analysis

Kinematics & Dynamics of Machines • Ontario Tech University • Team of 5 • Role: Lead Simulation & Modeling

Overview

As part of a kinematics course, we developed and analyzed a simplified model of a retractable landing gear system. The objective was to simulate the mechanical motion and validate its kinematic performance using vector loop equations and a sine-based actuator function. The final model was built and animated in Working Model and NX to observe the deployment, retraction, and tire center trajectory of the landing gear.

My Contributions

  • Led vector loop equation development and mobility analysis using 3-loop kinematic relationships
  • Built and animated both extended and retracted landing gear states in Working Model
  • Helped to model the landing gear in NX based on verified dimensions and timing constraints
