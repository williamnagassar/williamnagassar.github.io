Experimental Rocket for Jet Vane Development

Technology Development • Ontario Tech Space & Rocketry • Team Project

Overview

I designed the Mk I jet vane thrust vector control (JVTVC) test rocket as part of Ontario Tech Space & Rocketry’s Technology Development team. The goal was to create a dedicated testbed for evaluating low-speed jet vane control authority using solid propulsion. The rocket was built to intentionally minimize passive stability to allow observable influence from the jet vane mechanism during flight.

Key Design Highlights

  • Reduced fin area to avoid overstabilization during hover/ascent testing
  • Adjusted CG/CP relationship to balance control authority and flight safety
  • 3D-printed avionics bay lids, nosecone, and centering rings using PLA
  • Mass was adjusted to ensure sub-10 m/s impact speed as there would be no parachute in the design
  • Fastened using M4 bolts and heat-set inserts to reinforce critical areas and allow for easy disassembly
