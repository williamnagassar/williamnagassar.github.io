Exoskeleton Arm & Hand Grasper Design

Mechanical Design Course • Ontario Tech University • Team of 5 • Role: Lead Designer & Analyst

Overview

This project involved designing and prototyping a motorized exoskeleton arm capable of linear extension for assisted arm motion. The system was intended as a proof-of-concept for compact wearable assistive mechanisms, intended for elderly individuals or factory workers seeking lightweight load carrying assistance..

My Contributions

  • Created majority of the structural and force analysis using hand calculations and simulation tools.
  • Led the CAD development of the arm frame, grasper, and pulley mechanism in SolidWorks.
  • Designed key features for modularity and user adjustability.
  • Handled 30% of fabrication using both 3D-printed PLA and laser-cut aluminum components.

Technical Specifications

Actuator12V Linear Actuator, 50 mm stroke, 1000 N force
MaterialPLA for grasper & torso mount; 6061 aluminum for support structures
Return SystemElastic spring-loaded pulley with pre-tensioning slot
Testing StatusPrototype assembly completed; mechanical loading not performed due to course timeline
CAD SoftwareSolidWorks 2024
